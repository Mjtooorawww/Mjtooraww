Understanding Poverty

Exploring the causes, impacts, and solutions to global poverty

What is Poverty?

Poverty is the state of being extremely poor. It's a condition in which a person or community is deprived of, and lacks the essentials for a minimum standard of living, including food, clean water, basic education, healthcare, shelter, and clothing.

Extreme poverty is living on less than $1.90 a day. It affects hundreds of millions of people worldwide and remains one of the greatest challenges facing humanity.

Main Causes of Poverty

  • Lack of Education: Limited access to quality education reduces employment opportunities
  • Unemployment: Job scarcity and lack of economic opportunities
  • Health Issues: Illness and lack of healthcare prevent people from working
  • Discrimination: Prejudice based on gender, race, or caste
  • Poor Governance: Corruption and inefficient government policies
  • Natural Disasters: Environmental factors that destroy livelihoods
  • Limited Access to Resources: Shortage of land, water, and basic services

Solutions & How to Help

Addressing poverty requires a multi-faceted approach involving governments, organizations, and individuals:

  1. Education: Invest in quality education and vocational training
  2. Job Creation: Support entrepreneurship and create job opportunities
  3. Healthcare: Provide accessible and affordable healthcare services
  4. Social Safety Nets: Implement support programs for vulnerable populations
  5. Microfinance: Provide small loans to help people start businesses
  6. Infrastructure: Develop infrastructure to improve access to services
  7. Fair Trade: Support ethical trade practices and fair wages
